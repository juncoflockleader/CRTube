# PERSONA 4 — 16-BIT · YUKIKO
### A pixel-art short film. One HTML file. ~110 seconds + hold.
**Music:** the user's Persona 4 MIDI (electric piano, steady 95 BPM, ~64s), embedded
and looped seamlessly through the chiptune engine. Constant tempo → a *soft* lo-fi
drum grid (quiet kick, brushed hat) joins only in the town and festival scenes.
The whole film is warm and unhurried — nostalgia, not spectacle.

> They say if you stare at a switched-off TV at midnight, on a rainy night,
> you'll see your soulmate.

---

## Palette
Golden-hour gold and tatami brown, fog white, one TV-glow blue, and Yukiko's red —
headband, cardigan, kimono — always the reddest thing in frame. The color story:
blue loneliness → gold town → green riverbank → lantern warmth → white fog →
festival night → spring.

## Scenes

### 0 · TITLE — *silence*
A dark room. An old CRT television, glowing quietly. (On CRTube, of course.)
**PERSONA 4 — 16-BIT · YUKIKO** · *PRESS START*

### 1 · THE MIDNIGHT CHANNEL (12s) — *music enters, muffled and distant*
Rain on the window. A boy sits alone before the lit TV at 00:00.
On the fuzzy screen: the blurred outline of a girl.
> *Midnight. Rain. An old TV — and someone on the other side.*

### 2 · INABA (16s) — *the soft beat comes in*
Golden morning side-scroll through the shopping district — awnings, a red
vending machine, telephone poles with sagging wires, fog on the far hills.
Yu and Yukiko walk to school. A bicycle rattles past the other way.
Caption: `INABA — A TOWN IN THE FOG`

### 3 · THE RIVERBANK (16s) — *drums drop out; just the piano and the river*
The Samegawa flood plain. Gazebo, dragonflies, light on the water.
The two of them on the grass — and Yukiko loses it, one of her famous
laughing fits, rocking helplessly.
> YUKIKO: "Snrk — ahahaha! I'm sorry — I can't stop—!"

### 4 · THE AMAGI INN (16s) — *warm*
Dusk. The old inn glows lantern-orange; steam drifts from the spring behind.
Yukiko in a kimono on the engawa, Yu beside her, tea untouched.
> YUKIKO: "Everyone here raised me. This place is part of who I am."

### 5 · THE FOG (14s) — *lowpass veil; the piano sounds underwater*
The other world. White fog in slow sheets. A giant birdcage stands in the
haze, a girl inside, gold eyes watching from the mist. Yu reaches out —
and the bars fall away one by one. She walks out on her own.
> *She was never waiting to be rescued. She opened the door herself.*

### 6 · SUMMER FESTIVAL (18s) — *beat returns; the loop blooms*
Lantern strings, food stalls, friends in the background. Soft fireworks
open over the hills like slow flowers. Yukiko in a yukata, beside Yu.
> YUKIKO: "I'm staying in Inaba. Not because I have to — because I want to."
> YUKIKO: "...And because you're here."

### 7 · SPRING (hold) — *quiet, echoing, far away*
The station platform. A one-car train pulls out. Yukiko waves from the
platform edge until it's gone — then holds the frame: tracks, petals,
the fog lifting off the hills into blue.
> *Spring came. The fog never returned.*

## Emotional throughline
A stranger on a screen → a town that becomes home → laughter → belonging →
her own choice (the cage was never locked from outside) → saying it out loud →
and the sweet ache of leaving a place that now holds someone. Nostalgia is the
film's engine: every scene is something you can only visit again in memory.
