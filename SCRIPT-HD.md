# NieR:Automata — HI-BIT · ROUTE A + ENDING E
### The big one. 480×270, dithered gradients, multi-tone sprites. ~5:10 + hold.
**Music:** the Piano Collections "Weight of the World" MIDI (5:52, rubato) —
one continuous performance across the whole route. Battles get brightness and
octave shimmer, not drums; the piano is the narrator.
**References:** stage/boss order per the Route A walkthroughs
(gamepressure chapter index, fextralife Game Progress Route).

> This is the same story our first film told in 16-bit shorthand —
> now told properly: every stage, every boss, the whole descent.

## Scenes

### 0 · TITLE — ruined-city vista, hi-bit dithered sky
**NieR:Automata — HI-BIT | ROUTE A** · *PRESS START*

### 1 · PROLOGUE (12s) — *piano, sparse*
The monologue, typed over black.

### 2 · THE FACTORY (30s) — *verse*
Phase 1: flight units descend through flak over the factory stacks — side-scroll.
Phase 2: the catwalks. MARX — a Goliath swinging buzzsaw arms. Duck, dash, cut.
Phase 3: cornered by Goliaths, 2B and 9S trigger their black boxes —
a slow white bloom, and silence. `GLORY TO MANKIND.`

### 3 · THE BUNKER (16s) — *quiet*
Orbit. Earth in the window. The Commander. Rows of operators.
New bodies, no memories of the blast. Redeploy.

### 4 · CITY RUINS (28s) — *the WotW walk*
The long walk: four parallax layers of broken towers swallowed by green,
a moose grazing, stubby machines shuffling harmlessly. 9S falls in beside her.
The Resistance camp at the far end — Anemone's tents.

### 5 · THE DESERT (26s) — *low, strange*
Dunes to the horizon. Machines in masks, kneeling, rocking, chanting.
The pile of bodies convulses — and a golden figure sits up out of it, newborn.
ADAM. Bullets do nothing. A second figure pulls him under the sand. EVE.

### 6 · AMUSEMENT PARK (26s) — *waltz-dark*
Confetti from machines in party hats. A dead ferris wheel. In the theater:
SIMONE — an opera dress built of dolls, sweeping slow lasers like stage lights.
2B closes the distance between sweeps, and the dress comes down.

### 7 · PASCAL'S VILLAGE (14s) — *warm*
Machines that want nothing but peace. Pascal, and the little ones.

### 8 · GOLIATH INVASION (24s) — *verse, big*
The city ruins crack open: ENGELS — a walking building. Flight units strafe
its arm; it falls slow as a demolition. On the wing, 9S whoops. 2B does not.

### 9 · FLOODED CITY (22s) — *submerged, lowpass*
Drowned towers. A dive into the crater — down to the alien ship.
Inside: the aliens are husks, centuries dead. Standing over them, the brothers.
`THE MACHINES KILLED THEIR OWN GODS.`

### 10 · FOREST KINGDOM (20s) — *hushed*
Machine knights guard a castle grown through with trees. In the throne room,
a cradle: the Forest King, an infant core. A2 drops from the canopy —
one white cut — and is gone before the knights finish turning.

### 11 · COPIED CITY (22s) — *cold*
A city of white boxes, a photocopy of civilization. ADAM, barefoot, waiting:
he has read everything and understood nothing, and wants to feel the one
human thing left. 2B obliges. `"SO THIS IS DEATH."` He smiles going down.

### 12 · EVE (26s) — *the storm*
Machines everywhere, eyes red. EVE, marked with his brother's grief, tearing
the city apart with his hands. The last duel of Route A. When he falls,
the virus is already in 9S — red static crawling — and 2B holds him
as he asks her not to look. `IT ALWAYS ENDS LIKE THIS.`
Card: `ENDING A — FLOWERS FOR M[A]CHINES` · *but this isn't where it ends.*

### 13 · ENDING E — THE CREDITS (45s) — *the swell*
The credits are a bullet-hell, HD this time: the staff roll fights back,
the ship dies, restarts, dies. POD 042: "Do you wish for them to live?"
Rescue offers pour in. A wedge of allied ships. The wall of names breaks.
`"...YES. EVERYTHING."`

### 14 · DAWN (hold) — *music box*
The grass field at first light, three androids at rest, two pods deciding,
this time, to disobey. `A FUTURE IS NOT GIVEN TO YOU.` — fin —

## Notes
- Hi-bit = 480×270 canvas, dithered sky gradients (checker transitions),
  characters at ~44px with 8–12 tone palettes, disc-built props and bosses.
- All subtitles are in-world dialogue (characters, Pods, Operator 6O) — no
  narrator. Lines are kept to brief iconic fragments; a few are close
  reconstructions of the localization rather than verbatim.
- All flash-like moments (black box detonation, lasers, Excalibur-class beams)
  are continuous ramps — photosensitivity rules hold at any resolution.
